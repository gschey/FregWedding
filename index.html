Important Information

Goatfell Hike Details
  • Distance: Approximately 10 km (~6.2 mi) round trip
  • Elevation: 874m summit with ~890m elevation gain
  • Duration: Roughly 6-7 hours round trip depending on fitness and ceremony time
  • Difficulty: Moderate
  • What to bring: Hiking boots/other hiking footwear, waterproof jacket, layers (Scottish weather!), water, snacks, and sunscreen
  • Walking poles: Recommended, but not essential
  • Weather: Could be anything, so we will be checking regularly
  • Insect repellant: Is essential. Midges may well be present. Arran also has ticks which can carry Lyme disease.

Getting to Arran

The Isle of Arran is accessible by car and ferry. Here’s how to get there:

By Car & Ferry (Most Common)

Ardrossan to Brodick Ferry (CalMac)

From Glasgow: Drive south-west to Ardrossan (approximately 30 minutes from Glasgow city centre).

Ferry crossing: Approximately 55 minutes from Ardrossan to Brodick, Arran.

Cost: Standard car + driver plus foot passengers (prices vary by season and if you have a vehicle).

Booking: CalMac Ferries (book in advance, especially for cars in May).

Claonaig (Kintyre) to Lochranza Ferry (Seasonal)

Alternative route: Ferry from Claonaig on the Kintyre Peninsula to Lochranza in the north of Arran (April-September, weather dependent).

Crossing time: Approximately 30 minutes.

Booking: CalMac Ferries

Air Travel

Glasgow Airport

If flying into Scotland, Glasgow is the nearest major airport. From there, it’s a straightforward drive south-west to Ardrossan ferry terminal.

Tip: Car rental is recommended for exploring Arran, but taxis are available if needed.

Public Transport

Train + Ferry

Trains run to Ardrossan from Glasgow and other parts of Scotland. It’s then a short walk to the ferry terminal.

Bus + Ferry

Buses run to Ardrossan from Glasgow and other parts of Scotland. It’s then a short walk to the ferry terminal.

Things to Do on Arran

Arran is a wonderful island with plenty of activities and attractions. Whether you’re staying before or after the wedding, here’s some ideas:

Outdoor Activities

🏔️ Hiking & Mountain Walking
  • Goatfell: The iconic peak (obviously!)
  • Caisteal Abhail: Dramatic rock formations and views
  • Ben Nuis & Ben Bharrain: Beautiful ridges and summits
  • Coastal walks: Stunning beach and cliff walks throughout the island
  • Arran Way: 62 km coastal footpath perfect for day hikes
  • Dinosaur footprints: Need we say more?
  • Eas a' Chrannaig waterfall: This is a beautiful waterfall reached by a very pretty walk
  • King's Caves: Seafront caves with wall carvings from thousands of years ago. And little rock towers
🏖️ Beaches
  • Sannox Beach: A pretty beach with a lot of seaglass (if you look for it)
  • Brodick Beach: Sandy beach near Brodick town
  • Machrie Moor: Long sandy beach (where Arran Botanicals is located!)
  • Kildonan Beach: Peaceful south coast beach
  • Pirnmill Beach: Scenic northern beach with forest walks
⛵ Water Sports
  • Kayaking and canoeing
  • Fishing (sea and river)
  • Boat trips and wildlife viewing (seals, sea birds)
  • Windsurfing (seasonal)

Cultural & Historical Attractions

🏛️ Museums & Heritage Sites
  • Isle of Arran Heritage Museum: Learn about local history and archaeology
  • Brodick Castle: Historic castle with gardens overlooking the coast
  • Machrie Moor Stone Circles: Ancient standing stones with mysterious origins
  • Lochranza Castle: Picturesque ruins on the north coast

Culinary Experiences

🍽️ Food & Drink
  • Arran Botanicals: The famous beach cocktail bar! 🍸
  • Isle of Arran Distillery: Whisky distillery with tours and tastings
  • Blackwater Bakehouse: Honesty-box bakery with a really excellent oyster mushroom focaccia
  • Local restaurants: Fresh seafood and traditional Scottish fare throughout Brodick and villages

Relaxation & Wellness

🧘 Self-Care Activities
  • Auchrannie Resort spa facilities
  • Quiet beach time for reflection
  • Scenic picnics with island views
  • Photography opportunities galore

Wildlife & Nature

🦅 Nature Watching
  • Bird watching (golden eagles, sea eagles, seabirds)
  • Seal spotting (grey seals and harbour seals)
  • Otter opportunism (they usually hide out around Corrie)
  • Scenic photography of landscapes and wildlife
  • Botanical exploration (Arran is a paradise for plants!)

How fit do I need to be to hike Goatfell?

Goatfell is moderate to challenging. A reasonable level of fitness is needed, but it’s not extreme. The 874m elevation gain and ~6 hour round trip should be manageable for anyone with moderate hiking experience. If you have concerns, please get in touch - we can advise further or arrange alternatives.

What should I bring for the hike?

Essential:

  • Hiking footwear (boots or hiking-specific shoes) (waterproof recommended)
  • Waterproof jacket +/- waterproof trousers
  • Layers (fleece or insulating layer)
  • Water bottle (at least 1L)
  • Snacks/energy food
  • Sunscreen and hat
  • Basic first aid kit for your needs, eg blister plasters
  • Anti-insect spray/cream etc

Recommended:

  • Walking poles (if you usually use them)
  • Rucksack (20-30L is fine)
